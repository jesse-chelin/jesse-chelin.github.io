🔧 02 · Terminal SDK

Replacing a middleware dependency with something owned

Zeller's reliance on third-party middleware was a strategic risk — limited control, slow velocity, recurring costs. The business case landed with exec sponsorship, and a cross-functional team designed and shipped a proprietary Terminal SDK. The focus: developer experience. Specs, design workshops, feedback loops with early integration partners, and tight product-engineering collaboration ensured the team made the right trade-offs in the detail 🛠

The transferable skill: Build vs. buy evaluation and developer experience design — the same strategic lens applies to any platform or API product decision.

🛍 04 · POS Lite

I found the shortcut that got a POS system to market in months, not years

Merchants kept asking for an integrated POS, but a ground-up build was a 12+ month commitment. I worked with the engineering team to find a smarter path — uplifting our existing invoice item catalogue to support variants, modifiers, and discounts. I wrote the requirements, ran workshops with engineers on the item schema (designed for Square migration compatibility), and coordinated with design on the terminal interface. The result: a functional POS in a fraction of the time, shipped by a team that moved fast because they had the right context 🏗

The transferable skill: Reframing "build vs. buy" into "build vs. uplift" — finding existing assets that get you 80% of the way, then closing the gap with focused product work.

🍽 05 · Pay at Table

One product, three integration patterns — Pay at Table for hospitality

Pay at Table had to plug into three very different hospitality POS ecosystems — Tevalis (cloud API), H&L (cloud API with different auth), and Impos (physical bridge device on-premises). The product challenge: designing a single, consistent terminal experience regardless of what's running in the kitchen. That meant abstracting away backend complexity with integration engineers, negotiating technical constraints with each partner, and running pilot programmes with venue operators. Table turnover improved by up to 73% in early venues 🍷

The transferable skill: Designing unified experiences across fragmented integration landscapes — the product thinking that makes multi-partner ecosystems feel seamless to end users.

The integration strategy connecting 500+ POS apps to Zeller

Zeller's integration product strategy spans the build vs. partner decisions that determine how third-party POS systems connect to terminals. How I Work

My Approach to Product

The case studies above show what I've shipped. This is how I think — the principles and habits that transfer across any domain, team, or product.

🧩 Decompose, Then Sequence

Complex problems don't get solved in one swing. I break systems into layers, find the dependencies, and sequence delivery so teams ship incrementally without painting themselves into corners. The first question is always: what can we learn fastest?

🎬 Director, Not Dictator

I set creative direction and context. Engineers, designers, and analysts are experts at what they do — my job is to make sure they have the "why" so they can make brilliant "how" decisions.

🔍 Detail-Obsessed

I write the acceptance criteria. I review edge cases with engineers. I QA builds. Strategy without execution rigour is just a slide deck.

⚖️ Trade-offs Are the Job

Every product decision is a trade-off between speed, quality, scope, and risk. I make these explicit, quantify them where I can, and bring stakeholders along so decisions stick. No one gets surprised.

📊 Outcomes Over Output

Shipping features is easy. Moving metrics is hard. I anchor everything to measurable outcomes and make sure the team knows what success looks like before writing a line of code.

🛡 Constraints Are Features

Regulation, compliance, legacy systems — I treat constraints as design inputs, not blockers. Some of my best product work came from regulatory requirements everyone else saw as overhead.

🤝 Cross-Functional by Default

I sit at the intersection of engineering, design, commercial, and compliance. The best ideas come from bridging these perspectives, not working in silos.

🏗 Platforms Over Features

I bias toward building foundations that compound — item schemas that support future POS, SDKs that enable ecosystem growth, settlement engines that scale with volume.
